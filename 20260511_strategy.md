# Polymarket BTC Up/Down 15-Minute Strategy

*Date: 2026-05-11*

A structured framework for trading the 15-minute BTC binary markets on Polymarket, broken down by phases of the window lifecycle.

---

## Phase 0: Pre-Market Setup (Before Window Opens)

Continuous background tasks running 24/7:

1. **Price feed maintenance** — Keep a rolling buffer of BTC prices from Binance WebSocket (primary feed). Maintain at least the last 60 minutes of 1-second ticks for volatility/momentum calculations.

2. **Volatility regime tracking** — Continuously compute realized volatility (e.g., σ over last 5/15/60 min), ATR, and trend indicators (EMA fast/slow, momentum). This tells you *how movable* BTC is right now.

3. **Calibration table refresh** — Maintain a lookup table mapping `(time_remaining, |delta_from_strike|, volatility_regime) → P(win)`. This is your edge source — update it from historical 15m window outcomes.

---

## Phase 1: Window Opens (T-15:00 to T-13:00)

Identify the market and reference price:

1. **Fetch the new market** from Polymarket CLOB API as soon as the 15-min window opens. Extract:
   - `condition_id`, `token_id` (UP), `token_id` (DOWN)
   - Strike price (the BTC price at window open — this is the reference)
   - Resolution time (T+15:00)

2. **Skip the first ~60-120 seconds.** Order books are thin, prices are noisy, and "fair value" is essentially 50/50 because there's no signal yet. Entering here just pays spread to market makers.

3. **Confirm liquidity exists** — check bid/ask depth on both sides. If the book is thin (<$50 on best bid/ask), this window may not be tradeable.

---

## Phase 2: Signal Window (T-13:00 to T-3:00)

This is where most EV+ entries happen. You're looking for moments when your P(win) estimate exceeds the implied probability from market price by enough to cover edge thresholds.

For each tick (or each 1-2 second poll):

1. **Compute current state:**
   - `Δ = current_BTC - strike_price`
   - `t_remaining = resolution_time - now`
   - `σ_recent` = realized vol over last 2-5 min

2. **Estimate P(UP wins) using your model.** A simple baseline: assume BTC follows GBM, so `P(BTC_T > strike) = Φ((Δ + drift) / (σ·√t_remaining))`. Better: use your empirically calibrated lookup table conditioned on `(Δ, t_remaining, σ_regime)`.

3. **Read the market:** best bid and ask on UP and DOWN tokens. Implied prob of UP = mid-price of UP token.

4. **Compute edge:** `edge = P_model - P_market`. Account for the asymmetric payoff — buying at price `p` pays `1-p` if you win and loses `p` if you lose, so true breakeven needs `P_win > p` plus a buffer for fees and adverse selection.

5. **Apply strategy filters** (SCALP/MOMENTUM/FADE framework):
   - **SCALP** — small edges in low-vol regimes, exit quickly on mean reversion
   - **MOMENTUM** — enter in direction of strong directional move when `|Δ|` is growing and time is still ample
   - **FADE** — enter against the move when `|Δ|` is extreme but `t_remaining` is short enough that the move likely overshoots

6. **Gate checks before submitting any order:**
   - Edge > minimum threshold (e.g., 4-6% to cover spread + slippage + variance drag)
   - Entry price cap (don't buy above e.g. 0.85 — payoff ratio gets brutal)
   - Not already exposed in this window beyond position limit
   - Bankroll/Kelly check: position size = `f * bankroll` where `f` is a fractional Kelly (typically 0.1-0.25 of full Kelly given model uncertainty)

7. **Order placement:** GTC maker-first at the best bid. If unfilled after N seconds and signal still valid, either reprice up one tick or cancel. Avoid FAK/IOC against thin CLOB books — they fail too often.

---

## Phase 3: Position Management (After Fill)

1. **Track each open position** with: entry price, size, side, `P_at_entry`, time of entry.

2. **Re-evaluate every tick** — if `P_model` drops materially below `P_market` (your edge inverted), consider exiting via a sell order even at a small loss. Don't be a bagholder for 10 minutes hoping.

3. **Lock in profit on big moves** — if BTC has moved sharply in your direction and your token is now trading at 0.85+, the marginal `P_win` improvement from holding is tiny while downside is still substantial. Sell to a willing buyer.

---

## Phase 4: Closing Window (T-3:00 to T-0:30)

This is the highest-information phase — the strike outcome is becoming more deterministic.

1. **Tighten thresholds.** With less time for mean reversion, your model is more accurate, but so is the market's. The edge needed to enter scales up because slippage on exit is harder.

2. **Avoid new entries when `|Δ|` is very small and `t_remaining` < 90 seconds** — these are coinflips where the market is right and you're paying spread.

3. **FADE setups become attractive** when `Δ` has run hard in one direction with <2 min left — overshoots tend to mean-revert into resolution.

4. **No new positions after T-0:30.** Even if you fill, you can't exit if wrong; you're effectively going to expiry.

---

## Phase 5: Resolution (T-0:00 to T+5:00)

1. **Wait for Chainlink oracle** to post the resolution price. This can lag the actual T-0:00 by 30-180 seconds.

2. **Verify outcome** on-chain via the CTF contract (`0x4D97DCd97eC945f40cF65F87097ACe5EA0476045`).

3. **Hand off to redemption bot** — the split-bot design takes over here. The betting bot's job is done; redemption is a separate concern (the ERC-1155 stranding issue is handled in the redemption pipeline).

---

## Phase 6: Post-Window Learning Loop

1. **Log everything:** entry signal, model P, market P, fill price, outcome, PnL.

2. **Daily/weekly recalibration:** compare predicted P(win) vs realized win rate, bucketed by `(|Δ|, t_remaining, σ_regime)`. Adjust your lookup table.

3. **Watch for regime breaks** — a bot that worked in low-vol consolidation may bleed in trending regimes. Track PnL by detected regime and disable strategies in regimes where they're underperforming.

---

## The Hard Truth on EV

Given the asymmetric payoff structure causing EV-negative drift: the spreads and fees on these markets are real costs. Realistic profitable thresholds are tight. The two durable sources of edge are:

1. **A better short-horizon BTC price model** than the market median participant
2. **Speed advantage** over slower bots when news/order-flow shocks hit

Pure technical signals on a 15-min binary are crowded — calibration quality and execution discipline matter more than which strategy bucket fires.

## Quick Reference: Phase Timeline

| Phase | Time | Primary Action |
|-------|------|----------------|
| 0 | Always-on | Maintain price feed, vol regime, calibration table |
| 1 | T-15:00 → T-13:00 | Fetch market, skip noise, confirm liquidity |
| 2 | T-13:00 → T-3:00 | Main entry window — compute edge, gate, place GTC |
| 3 | After any fill | Manage open positions, exit on inverted edge |
| 4 | T-3:00 → T-0:30 | Tighter thresholds, FADE setups, no entries after T-0:30 |
| 5 | T-0:00 → T+5:00 | Wait for oracle, verify outcome, hand off to redemption bot |
| 6 | Post-window | Log, recalibrate, monitor regime |

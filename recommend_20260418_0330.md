## Recommendation 1 — Raise the Delta signal threshold to 0.20%

**Impact: highest. Retrospective P&L: +$36.17 vs −$113.27.**

Every losing trade in the session had `|Delta|` between 0.15% and 0.19%. Trades with stronger deltas were perfect.

| Signal filter | N | Wins | Losses | P&L |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| All filled trades | 24 | 17 | 7 | −$97.46 |
| `|Delta|` ≥ 0.20% | 4 | 4 | 0 | **+$36.17** |
| `|Delta|` < 0.20% | 20 | 13 | 7 | −$133.63 |

**Action:**
- In `strategy_v2.py`, raise `DELTA_MIN` from ~0.15% to `0.20%` for SCALP strategy
- Consider 0.25% for MOMENTUM (already higher-conviction by design)
- N=4 is small — monitor live and re-tune after 30–50 filled trades

**Why this works:** Weak delta signals don't have enough predictive power to overcome the 20% adverse implied-probability cost at $0.79 entry prices. The model's edge is real but concentrated in stronger-signal regimes.

## Recommendation 2 — Cap max entry price at $0.80

**Impact: high. Eliminates the $0.89 tail-loss exposure.**

EV per dollar risked by entry price:

| Entry | N | Win rate | Break-even WR needed | EV per $1 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $0.79 | 11 | 64% | 79% | **−19%** |
| $0.89 | 7 | 71% | 89% | **−20%** |
| $0.68–$0.85 (mid) | 6 | 83% | 68–85% | **+20% avg** |

The $0.89 band is the silent killer. 71% hit rate feels good, but you lose 89¢ per share when missing and win only 11¢ when hitting. One $33 loss wipes out eight $4 wins.

**Action:**
- Set hard `MAX_BUY_PRICE = 0.80` in config
- If best ask is above $0.80 and your max_bid would be ≥ $0.80, skip the window entirely
- Do NOT just lower the bid — that crushes fill rate without fixing the EV problem at prices above $0.80

**Side benefit:** Your MOMENTUM strategy posted 5 signals at $0.89, all unfilled. Tightening here costs almost nothing in real trades.

## Recommendation 4 — Recalibrate P(win) model

**Impact: structural. The model is 15–19 percentage points overconfident.**

| Model says P(win) = | Actual hit rate | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| 0.80 | 64% (7/11) | **−16pp** |
| 0.90 | 71% (5/7) | **−19pp** |

This matters because Kelly sizing trusts P(win) directly. Overconfident P(win) → oversized positions → amplified losses on miscalibrated trades.

**Actions (pick one or combine):**

**A. Shrinkage toward 50%:**
```python
p_calibrated = 0.5 + 0.7 * (p_model - 0.5)
# Example: p_model=0.80 → p_calibrated=0.71
#          p_model=0.90 → p_calibrated=0.78
```

**B. Reduce Kelly fraction:**
- Current: quarter-Kelly (per your earlier design)
- Change to eighth-Kelly until 50+ filled trades let you re-fit

**C. Dynamic throttle based on realized WR:**
```python
rolling_wr = wins_last_20 / 20
implied_wr = mean(p_win_last_20)
if rolling_wr < implied_wr - 0.10:
    kelly_fraction *= 0.5  # halve sizing until calibration recovers
```

Recommend B + C combined for immediate safety, then retrain the base P(win) model once 100+ trades are logged.

## Recommendation 5 — Adverse-fill protection on GTC orders

**Impact: medium. Reduces winner's-curse contamination.**

The bot posts GTC bids at $0.79 against best asks of $0.99 (a 20¢ gap). Fill rate at this price is 38%. When fills occur, they're disproportionately because the market moved against the position — classic adverse selection on maker orders.

**Action:** Poll the book every 15 seconds after posting the GTC bid. Cancel if:
- Best ask drops below your bid level by ≥ 2¢ (e.g., you bid $0.79, ask is now $0.77) — signal has reversed
- Best bid crosses above your level — someone else has a stronger view and is paying up
- Book depth on your side disappears (e.g., single-ask $0.99 anomaly returns)

Pseudocode:
```python
async def monitor_gtc(order_id, posted_price, direction):
    while order_still_open(order_id):
        await asyncio.sleep(15)
        book = fetch_book(token_id)
        if direction == "Up":
            if book.best_ask < posted_price - 0.02:
                cancel_order(order_id)
                log("Adverse fill protection triggered")
                return
        # mirror logic for Down
```
